Base 

If I counted the times I have been called Angry
Bossy
Mean
Or scary
In my lifetime, I would start to hate me
Where single strokes paint the colors of my new key
To a reality predestined for me
Where queens are all white and
and black is just the unwanted stain
that gets left out during winter's harshest rain’s
and feels every word of hate you spill into Black’s colonized brain
Black has curly roots that reaches deep into dark black ink
When you comb out each kink
It turns out ever so poofier than your wildest dreams
Where no matter how many times you try to be like them
You push yourself into a constructed false reality
Where no matter how many times you swear the cream will erase away the black
You never admit to never being able to change the colors God gave you
God gave you a base
A base so deep everything you entertain falls in the mercy of your kingdom
A base so deep it stores all the pain and turns it into wisdom
It piles up before the clouds ever gets the chance to read its deep melody
Before your melanin gets the chance to sing
A song called Beauty
A song that sings about your kingdom
How your voice is history enough
And every sound deserves an orchestra at its feet
In your kingdom, you'll finally be the standard
In your kingdom, you will finally be excellence
Your feet will stomp the old freedom spiritual
Your legs will be as wavy as the dark waters of your passage
Your torso will sink as deep as the beat that recites what it means to be a woman
Your chin will rise above the skyscrapers that corporate America used to block your sun
the skyscrapers that corporate America used to erase your sun
your eyes will see through the stories of every black girl who's not good enough
Your ears will hear the resonating rings that started 4 score and 7 years ago
And your arms will extend out showing your battle scars
Your tissue that hangs thin at expectations of beauty
they will hang like the feathers of the most exotic bird
They will scream this is what pain looks like
They will wail this is what America looks like
This is what it means to strike back at the bleached hands of expectation
This must be how it feels to reacquire power in this infestation
My black will continue to exhilarate the bright future that is my tomorrow
It will suffocate the shackles you have inscribed with sorrow
It will stand up
Reach
Quiver at the freedom of regaining touch
And your hands
Your hands
will be able to write their own history
This it what it finally means to feel
Now let's start to listen
